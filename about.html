Party History

Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP)

The Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP) was founded on September 1, 1978 by President Ziaur Rahman. The party emerged with the vision of strengthening the independence, sovereignty, and democratic values of Bangladesh.

BNP played an important role in shaping the political landscape of the country. It advocates nationalism, economic development, and the protection of democratic institutions.

Over the years, the party has participated in several national elections and has governed the country multiple times, implementing various economic and social reforms aimed at development and national unity.

Ideology & Vision

Nationalism

BNP believes in strong national identity and sovereignty. The party promotes the values of independence and unity among the people of Bangladesh.

Democracy

The party supports a democratic system where people's rights, freedom of speech, and political participation are respected and protected.

Economic Development

BNP focuses on economic growth, employment opportunities, industrial development, and improving the living standards of citizens.

Constitution

Party Constitution

The constitution of the Bangladesh Nationalist Party provides the organizational structure and guiding principles of the party. It defines the responsibilities of leadership, party members, and different committees within the organization.

It also outlines the democratic procedures for leadership selection, policy formulation, and internal governance.

The constitution ensures transparency, accountability, and discipline within the party organization while maintaining its commitment to democratic values.
